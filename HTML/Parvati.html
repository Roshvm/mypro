Prayers of Parvati

I was sitting by the banks of the Parvati
when a prayer came fluttering in the wind
it was softer than a whisper
fainter than the rustling of leaves
The gurgle of the river didnt disturb it
nor did the song of the Blue whisling thrush
Was it meant for me by the lips that birthed it?
I would never know
and shall not seek for that knowledge
for I badly needed the prayer
and couldnt afford to pass it on.
Far away, in a Himalayan shrine
surrounded by Buransh blooms
the wind kissed the prayer flags goodbye
before it set out on a journey
one of no return or destination
